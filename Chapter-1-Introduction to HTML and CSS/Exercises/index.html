Spaghetti Carbonara

What is Spaghetti Carbonara?

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese (Pecorino Romano or Parmesan), pancetta or guanciale, and freshly ground black pepper. It's creamy, rich, and quick to prepare â€” a perfect weeknight meal made with simple ingredients and full of authentic Roman flavor.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 whole eggs + 2 egg yolks
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano
  • Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Salt (for boiling pasta)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  2. In a skillet over medium heat, cook pancetta or guanciale until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, yolks, cheese, and lots of black pepper until well combined.
  4. Quickly toss the hot pasta with the pancetta and its rendered fat.
  5. Remove from heat and slowly mix in the egg mixture, stirring quickly to avoid scrambling. Add reserved pasta water as needed to create a creamy sauce.
  6. Serve immediately, topped with more cheese and black pepper.
